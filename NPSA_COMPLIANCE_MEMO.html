SendR NPSA Compliance Memo (Internal Audit Trail)

Date: 2026-07-05 Subject: Compliance with the National Payment System Act (NPSA) Prepared for: MzansiCash Legal & Operations

1. Executive Summary

This memo serves as an internal audit trail regarding the architecture of the SendR application and its compliance with the National Payment System Act (Act 78 of 1998) ("NPSA").

The core technical risk addressed by this memo is the requirement that only licensed entities (banks, clearing houses, designated system operators) may clear and settle payments in South Africa.

2. Architecture & Funds Flow

SendR operates strictly as a Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) and Merchant Interface, not as a clearing house or an issuer of e-money.

2.1 The Paystack Subaccount Model

We utilize Paystack South Africa (a licensed and authorized payment processor) to handle the actual movement of funds.

  • When a user signs up on SendR, we dynamically create a Paystack Subaccount linked to their verified South African bank account.
  • When User A sends money to User B, SendR instructs the Paystack API via a secure backend to charge User A's card and route the funds via Paystack's Split Payment feature.
  • Paystack settles the funds directly into User B's subaccount, depositing the principal directly into User B's actual bank account, minus the platform margin which is settled to MzansiCash's primary Paystack account.

2.2 Pending Invites

If User A sends money to a phone number that is not yet registered on SendR:

  • We execute a standard Paystack charge on User A, but the funds settle entirely into MzansiCash's primary Paystack account holding account.
  • A cryptographic token is generated.
  • When User B registers and links their bank account, the system uses Paystack API to push the held funds from MzansiCash's Paystack balance to User B's newly created subaccount.

3. Regulatory Conclusion

Because MzansiCash utilizes a licensed third-party (Paystack) to process the authorization, clearing, and settlement of all transactions:

  1. MzansiCash (Pty) Ltd does not require a banking license.
  2. MzansiCash (Pty) Ltd does not operate as a Designated Settlement System.
  3. The platform operates within the legal bounds of a third-party payment provider / merchant reseller structure.
