SendR POPIA & PAIA Manual

Prepared in accordance with section 51 of the Promotion of Access to Information Act, No. 2 of 2000 ("PAIA") and to address the requirements of the Protection of Personal Information Act, No. 4 of 2013 ("POPIA").

1. Introduction

MzansiCash (Pty) Ltd ("the Company") operates the SendR mobile application, a peer-to-peer payment platform. This manual is published in terms of Section 51 of PAIA and provides an outline of the types of records held by the Company and explains how one may submit requests for access to these records in terms of PAIA.

It also outlines our framework for processing personal information in compliance with POPIA.

4. POPIA Compliance Framework

The Company adheres to the 8 Conditions for Lawful Processing of Personal Information as stipulated by POPIA:

  1. Accountability: The Company ensures that the conditions for lawful processing are complied with.
  2. Processing Limitation: Personal information is collected minimally, lawfully, and with consent (e.g., location data is only captured with explicit mobile OS consent).
  3. Purpose Specification: Data is collected for explicit purposes: facilitating P2P payments, fraud prevention (Device Binding), and regulatory compliance (FICA).
  4. Further Processing Limitation: Data is not processed in ways incompatible with the original purpose.
  5. Information Quality: The Company allows users to update their information to maintain accuracy.
  6. Openness: This manual and our Privacy Policy are publicly available.
  7. Security Safeguards: The Company employs Firebase App Check, JWT authentication, and encrypted databases (Supabase) to secure data.
  8. Data Subject Participation: Users have the right to request access to and deletion of their data.

6. How to Request Access to Records (PAIA Section 51(1)(e))

To request access to a record held by the Company, the requester must:

  1. Complete the prescribed Form C (available on the Information Regulator's website).
  2. Submit the form to the Information Officer via email (support@mzansicash.co.za).
  3. Provide sufficient detail to enable the Information Officer to identify the record and the requester.
  4. Indicate which form of access is required.
  5. Specify a postal address or email address in South Africa.
  6. Pay the prescribed request fee (if applicable, as gazetted).

The Company will process the request within 30 days, unless an extension is legally permitted.

A copy of this manual is available upon request from the Information Officer and is accessible within the SendR application and on our website.
